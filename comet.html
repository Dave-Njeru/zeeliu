Comet App

UX/UI Design

Overview

Comet is a mobile app for people who face dilemma and frustration when chossing gifts for friends and family and receiving gifts from other people. It is a platform that provides a more genuine gifting experience, which makes the process more delightful and rewarding.
Project Type:Individual project
Timeline:March 2020 - May 2020
Tools:Figma, Illustrator, Photoshop, Miro

Solutions

01. Provide anonymous access to friends' wishlist

02. Allow gifts receivers and and edit their wishlists

01. Validating the problem

Personal Experience

Between March 2019 - March 2020, my expense on gifts purchasing is $1139.80. and I received 16 gifts in total from friends and family in the same period. Among the 16 items, only 3 are what I consider items I genuinely enjoy, and 1 is used daily.

Are wasted money and unwanted items the common problems people are facing in gifting situations?
Could there be a better way to play the game of "guess what I like"?

Asking Around

With my questions in mind, I talked with people around me with the curiosity of whether the problems regarding giving and receiving gifts are shared among different people:

"I think the whole process is nerve wracking and frustrating for most people of the time."
--- Jordan, 26, consultant

"The thing is, I spend money and time to get them something they probably don't like ... I usually don't really need the stuff I get for my birthday either."
--- Anonymous, 32, game streamer

"I have a lot of stuff at home from friends are not needed, but it's not nice to throw them away..."
--- Dom, 24, Student

Initial Research Objective

Giving and receiving gifts has been a meaningful social interaction that maintains and strengthens relationships.
What has caused frustation in gift exchanges in the modern world?
How can we optimize the process to where people can enjoy giving and receiving gifts while improving their relationship on the way?

Survey Analysis

In a survey conducted among 35 people in all ages, genders, income and locations. The following problems are proved to be the most common sources of frustration when it comes to giving and receiving gifts:

87%
of the participants think the most frustrating aspect of buying gifts is people pretending to like what they bought and never use/ wear/ showcase them.
80%
of the participants think "To buy what the receivers truly need/ like" is more important than "surprising them with the unknown."
65%
of the participants, in a hypothetical scenario, decided to pool money with another friend to buy the receiver the receiver the expensive item that he/she really wants, instead of buying something else.

01. Validating the problem

Majority of the people enjoy straightforward communications and genuine outcome when it comes to gifting. But the tradition of gifting culture of surpirse made it into a guessing game that's time/ money consuming.

Task Flows

Wireframe & Testing

To validate the usability of the Wireframe, I invited 7 candidates to participate paper prototype testing.

Results:

  • 6/7 participants successully completed both tasks
  • 6/7 participants understood the context of the app
  • 4/7 participants provided comments and suggestions
  • 1/7 participants ran into confunsion regarding the purpose of the app

Areas For Improvements:

  • CTA buttons can be more visually prominent
  • Optimize the content and copies to be more explanatory for users

A/B Testing

I gathered comments from 5 participants regarding the visual layouts and task flows in several user testing interviews. To validate the changed screens' improvement, I designed A/B Testing surveys and updated the following screens.

06. Reflections

Unsure? Do more Testings
The insights I gathered from user testings brought my designs from intuition to implementations. Research and prototyping are ongoing processes that can always be closer to the best by validating design and ideas with the users.
Deliver the context
For an app that doesn't have a context that the audience is familiar with, it's crucial to use copy texts and visual design to deliver the contexts and demonstrate and usability map.
